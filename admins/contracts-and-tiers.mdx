## What is a contract?

A contract defines how commissions are calculated for a partner or sales person. Each user must be assigned a contract for commissions to be calculated when their sales are approved.

## Creating a contract

1. Go to **Contracts** in the sidebar
2. Click **New Contract**
3. Fill in:
   - **Contract name** — e.g. "Standard Partner 10%"
   - **Effective date** — when this contract applies from
   - **Commission structure** — choose one of the types below
4. Click **Save**

## Commission structure types

### Flat rate
A fixed percentage applied to every deal value.

**Example:** 10% on all deals → a ₹1,00,000 deal earns ₹10,000 commission.

### Tiered rate
Different percentages apply as the partner's cumulative sales volume crosses thresholds.

**Example:**
| Volume | Rate |
|---|---|
| ₹0 – ₹5,00,000 | 8% |
| ₹5,00,001 – ₹20,00,000 | 10% |
| Above ₹20,00,000 | 12% |

### Commission cap and minimum

You can optionally set:
- **Cap** — maximum commission per deal (e.g. no more than ₹50,000 per deal)
- **Minimum** — minimum commission per deal even if percentage is lower

## Assigning a contract to a partner

After creating the contract, go to the partner's profile and select it in the **Contract** field, or assign it directly from the Contracts page.

## Scenarios

For more complex, non-standard commission structures, use **Scenarios** (sidebar → Scenarios). Scenarios allow rule-based commission logic with conditions such as product type, region, or deal size.

<Note>
  Changes to a contract do not retroactively affect already-approved commissions. New commissions use the contract active at the time of approval.
</Note>
